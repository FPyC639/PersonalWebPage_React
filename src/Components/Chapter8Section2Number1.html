Chapter 8

Section 2

Problem 1

To begin the problem one of the first steps that one has to is to convert the following equation into matrix format:

`\left\{\array{\frac{dx}{dt}=x+2y \\ \frac{dy}{dt}= 4x+3y }{c}\right.`

Converting this to the following format:

`\equation{\frac{d}{dt}} \pmatrix{x \\ y}=\pmatrix{1 & 2 \\4 & 3}\pmatrix{x \\ y}`

`\mathbf{\vec{X'}}=\pmatrix{1 & 2 \\ 4 & 3}\mathbf{\vec{X}}`

The next phase of solving the problem would be to do the following:

`\begin{align*}\det(A-\lambda I)&=0 \\ \det(\pmatrix{1 & 2 \\ 4 & 3}- \lambda\pmatrix{1&0\\0&1})&=0 \end{align*}`

`\begin{align*} \det(\pmatrix{1-\lambda & 2 \\ 4 & 3-\lambda})&=0 \\ (1-\lambda)(3-\lambda) - 8&=0\end{align*}`

`\begin{align*} \lambda^2-4\lambda-5&=0 \\ (\lambda-5)(\lambda+1) &= 0 \\ \lambda&=-1,5 \end{align*}`

These are your eigenvalues and the next part of the problem would be to find the eigenvectors for the problem:

`\begin{align*} \pmatrix{1-\lambda & 2 \\ 4 & 3 -\lambda}\Bigg\vert_{\lambda=5}\pmatrix{k_1 \\ k_2}&= \pmatrix{0 \\ 0} \\ \pmatrix{-4 & 2 \\ 4 & -2}\pmatrix{k_1 \\ k_2}&= \pmatrix{0 \\ 0}\end{align*}`

`\begin{align*} -4k_1+2k_2&=0 \\ k_1&=1 \\ k_2&=2 \end{align*}`

We have just arrived at our first eigenvector which is the following:

`c_1\pmatrix{1 \\ 2}e^{5t}`

Now to arrive at the other eigen vector we do the following:

`\begin{align*} \pmatrix{1-\lambda & 2 \\ 4 & 3 -\lambda}\Bigg\vert_{\lambda=-1}\pmatrix{k_1 \\ k_2}&= \pmatrix{0 \\ 0} \\ \pmatrix{2 & 2 \\ 4 & 4}\pmatrix{k_1 \\ k_2}&= \pmatrix{0 \\ 0}\end{align*}`

`\begin{align*} 2k_1+2k_2&=0 \\ k_1&=-1 \\ k_2&=1 \end{align*}`

Then the second eigenvector is the following:

`c_2\pmatrix{-1\\1}e^{-t}`

Thus the combined solution is the following:

`\mathbf{\vec{x}}=c_1\pmatrix{1 \\ 2}e^{5t}+c_2\pmatrix{-1\\1}e^{-t}`
