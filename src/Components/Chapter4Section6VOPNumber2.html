Chapter 4.7 Variation of Parameters

Number 2

The problem statement is the following:

\begin{equation} y''+ y =\tan(x) \end{equation}

Now one can refer to the auxiliary eqaution to get the associated homogenous:

\begin{align*}m^2+1&=0 \\ m^2&=-1 \\ m=\pm i \end{align*}

From here we get the homogenous part of our solution which is the following:

\begin{equation}y_h=c_1\cos(x)+c_2\sin(x)\end{equation}

Now we can setup the Wronskian for the problem, based of the homogeous part, and nonhomgenous part:

\begin{align*}W&= \begin{vmatrix}\cos(x) & \sin(x) \\ -\sin(x) & \cos(x) \end{vmatrix} \\ W&=\cos^2(x)+\sin^2(x)=1 \end{align*}

Unlike in problem 1, this one will be solved using the Wronskians:

\begin{align*}u'_1(x)&=\frac{W_1}{W}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}0 & \sin(x) \\ \tan(x) & \cos(x) \end{vmatrix} }{1} \\ &=-\tan(x)\sin(x) \end{align*}

Now integrating $u'_1(x)$

\begin{align*} \int \frac{-\sin^2(x)}{\cos(x)}dx&=\int -\frac{(1-\cos^2(x)}{\cos(x)} \\ \int -(\sec(x)-\cos(x)) dx &= -\ln{(\lvert \sec(x)+\tan(x) \rvert)}+\sin(x) \end{align*}

The next part of the problem to do is solve for $u'_2$ by setting up the next Wronskian:

\begin{align*}u'_2(x) &= \frac{W_2}{W}=\frac{\begin{vmatrix}\cos(x) & 0 \\ -\sin(x) & \tan(x) \end{vmatrix}}{1}\\&=\sin(x)\end{align*}

\begin{equation}u_2(x)=\int u'_2 \ dx = \int \sin(x) dx = -\cos(x)\end{equation}

\begin{equation}y_p=y_1u_1+y_2u_2=\cos(x)(\sin(x)-\ln{(\lvert \sec(x) + \tan(x) \rvert)}+\cos(x)\sin(x)\end{equation}

Therefore that means then that the solution to the whole equation is the particular plus the homogenous:

\begin{equation}y=y_p+y_h= c_1\cos(x)+c_2\sin(x)+\cos(x)(\sin(x)-\ln{(\lvert \sec(x) + \tan(x) \rvert)}+\cos(x)\sin(x)\end{equation}
